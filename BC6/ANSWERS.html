Two different NT ranges

Top  Previous  Next

 

If you want to play two different NT ranges, here's how to set up your CC.  Show the primary range on the top line of the 1NT HCP range section. Then set the secondary 1NT range on the second line. Next, indicate when you wish to use the secondary NT range by checking the requisite boxes.  For example, if you play 15-17 NTs in all situations except in 1st or 2nd seat when you are not vulnerable, set the primary range to 15-17.  Then set the secondary range to 12-14, or whatever. Next check both "Non-Vul" and "1st/2nd."  Note, if you donâ€™t check any box, Bridge Captain will never use the secondary range.

 

Time shown in the upper right

Top  Previous  Next

 

The time shown in the upper right is the time it took Bridge Captain to bid and play the hand in the Closed Room. The more thinking time you've selected (from Preferences), the more Bridge Captain thinks and the longer it takes to play a hand.

 

Thinking time

Top  Previous  Next

 

The most important factors affecting the computer's skill at bridge playing are the speed of the computer and the amount of time spent determining the best play. You can control the amount of time Bridge Captain will spend thinking about the best play.

 

In order to provide the highest level of competition with a very short "thinking" period, Bridge Captain can use double dummy defense, after the opening lead has been made. By "double dummy" this means the computer "peeks" at all four hands in order to decide the best play.

 

Playing Tournament hands

Top  Previous  Next

 

Bridge Captain has the ability to read PBN (Portable Bridge Notation) files. PBN is a common format used by many bridge programs to allow the easy transfer of bridge files between programs. Most importantly, records of many of the international bridge events are stored in PBN format and are available for download from the internet.

 

Downloading PBN files

The best place to download THOUSANDS of tournament files is at

 

http://www.sarantakos.com/bridge/vugraph.html

 

I recommend storing the files at the default location of:

{for WinXP}

C:\Documents and Settings\YourUserName\Application Data\Tournaments

 

{for Vista}

C:\Users\YourUserName\AppData\Roaming\Bridge Captain\Ver 6\Tournaments

 

It's helpful to store all of the files in a logical folder structure that will enable you to find files easily. Here's a picture of my structure for 2002.

 

Playing Tournament Hands

Now you're ready to play some tournament hands. Start Bridge Captain and click "Select Bridge File" from Dealer Options.  Click "Select Folder" and now "navigate" to the correct folder, clicking the + signs besides a folder to open it. Remember the files are stored at

 

{for Vista}

C:\Users\YourUserName\AppData\Roaming\Tournaments\saved deals\deal library\2002\etc.

 

Once you've navigated to a folder with no "+" next to it, you've finally gotten to a folder with tournament files in it. Click the OK button.  Bridge Captain will remember this address so you won't have to navigate all the way to it once again.

 

All of the PBN files in that folder will now appear. Click on one of the files and you will see numbers for all the hands in the file, displayed on the right. While you could go directly to one of those hands, I recommend that you now click "1st Board not played".  Bridge Captain will start you off playing those hands.  Enjoy )

 

The next time you click Select Bridge File, Bridge Captain will automatically position you to the last folder where you selected a tournament file.

 

As tournaments get completed during the year, more and more hands are posted to the above uorogon.edu web location.

 

Comparing Tournament Results

After you finish playing a tournament hand click "Score Book" to see the results of all the tables that played this hand. To see the results of a specific table, click on that table's results (to highlight that table) and then click either the Table Results tab, or the "Selected Table" button. Other tabs summarize the results for the hands stored in this file.
