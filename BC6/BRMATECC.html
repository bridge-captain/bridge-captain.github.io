2NT = Transfer to 3À

Top  Previous  Next

 

After a 1NT opening, some partnerships play that a 2NT response is a transfer to 3♣; it is not invitational to 3NT. In this case, responder should have one of the following two hands:

Weak with long clubs. Responders passes opener's 3♣ bid.

Slam interest with 4441 distribution. Responder discloses the exact shape by bidding the singleton, unless it is a club, in which case he bids 3NT. For example, after 1N-2N!-3♣ a 4♥ bid shows 4144 distribution.

 

To invite game after partner opens 1NT, responder bids Stayman, and then rebids 2NT to announce an invitational balanced hand. Opener passes with a minimum, or bids 3NT with a maximum. As in real life, BridgeMate pushes to 3NT with anything but a bare minimum!

 

If you play 2NT = transfer to 3♣:

Right click your mouse to reveal the popup menu.

Left click 2NT → 3♣.

4th Suit Forcing - 1 round

Top  Previous  Next

 

When playing "4th Suit Forcing" a bid of the 4th suit by responder generally shows 5 cards in responder's first suit, and asks opener to show support with 3 or more.  The values expressed by the 4th suit forcing bid must be checked on the CC.

 

For example, 1♥-1♠-2♣-2♦!   Diamonds is the "fourth" suit bid.  The bid of the 4th suit is artificial and does not imply any length or strength in that suit.  In this example, responder might even be void in diamonds!

Brozel

Top  Previous  Next

 

Brozel is a convention used to describe a two suited hand after

an opponent opens 1NT:

Dbl shows ANY single suited hand,

2♣ shows both clubs and hearts,

2♦ shows both diamonds and hearts,

2♥ shows both hearts and spades,

2♠ shows spades and an undisclosed minor suit.

 

Playing Brozel, the overcaller shows a single long suit by doubling 1NT. Partner is forced to bid 2♣. The overcaller then can pass, showing a club suit, or next bid the long suit.

 

Thus, the overcaller can not make a penalty double of 1NT.

 

Cansino

Top  Previous  Next

 

Cansino is used to describe various hands after an opponent opens 1NT:

Dbl shows ANY 2 suited hand.

2C shows 3 suits, including clubs.

2D shows 3 suits; specifically diamonds, hearts, and spades.

2H just shows hearts.

2S just shows spades.

 

After a double, partner bids the longer minor suit. Overcaller passes or corrects the contract to the cheaper suit. Partner may bid 2NT to show a good hand.

 

Thus, the overcaller can not make a penalty double of 1NT.

 

Note: I have learned that there are many different version called "Cansino".  This version was published by Terence Reese.

 

Cappelletti/Hamilton

Top  Previous  Next

 

Hamilton, a.k.a. Cappelletti, is used after an opponent opens 1NT:

Dbl is penalty oriented.

2♣ shows a hand with one long suit.

2♦ shows both Major suits.

2♥ shows hearts and a minor suit.

2♠ shows spades and a minor suit.

2NT shows both minor suits.

 

After the 2♣ overcall, partner typically bids 2♦. Overcaller then passes or corrects the contract to the long suit.

 

After the 2♥ or 2♠ overcall, Advancer bids 2NT to find out overcaller's minor suit.

 

Card Showing or Action Doubles

Top  Previous  Next

 

An Action Double is made by responder in the pass out seat, when his/her partner was the opener. This bid denies support for opener's suit, but reveals about 9-12 points and no clear cut action. Opener will often pass the Action Double, converting it into a penalty double.

 

Checkback Stayman

Top  Previous  Next

 

Like New Minor Forcing, Checkback Stayman seeks to discover a 5-3 Major suit fit.

Unlike NMF, the reresponse of 2♣ is always used, even if the opening bid was 1♣.  Thus 1♣-1♠-1N-2♣! is "Checkback", does not imply club support, and is forcing.

 

Opener response priorities are as follows:

1. Show 3 card support for responder's Major,

2. Show 4 cards in the other Major suit, or

3. Bid 2♦ to deny either of the above.

 

When playing Checkback, the 2♣ bid always shows at least invitational values, and is the only forcing bid after opener's 1NT rebid.  Thus a reresponse of another suit at the 2 level shows less than invitational.  In particular, a reresponse of 3♣ is NON-FORCING; i.e. 1♣-1♠-1N-3♣! is non-forcing. (as is 1♦-1♠-1N-3♣).

 

Control Bids Show Second Round

Top  Previous  Next

 

First round controls are aces and voids. Second round controls are kings and singletons. When probing for slam, BridgeMate shows first round controls only, unless you previously selected the second round controls option. (CC section Other Conventional Calls). Then BridgeMate shows both first and second round controls. If the bidding shows that the partnership is missing a second round control, BridgeMate will not make any subsequent control bids.

Constructive Raise

Top  Previous  Next

 

When playing constructive raises a simple raise shows 8-10 points and at least three trump (exactly three trump if playing Bergen Raises).

 

Holding fewer than 8 points, with support, responder first bids a forcing1ΝΤ, and then shows a preference for opener's major suit. Opener cannot tell if responder is weak with three-card support, or has up to 10 points with only two-card support.

 

Conventional Raise, Jacoby 2NT

Top  Previous  Next

 

If you play Jacoby 2NT, select Conv. Raise 2NT from your CC.

 

With this convention, 2NT in response to an opening 1♥ or 1♠ bid shows four or five-card support, a balanced hand and 13 or more points. It denies a good five-card suit.

 

The 2NT bid is forcing and it asks opener to bid a void or singleton. Not holding a void or singleton, BridgeMate will rebid:

four in the major with fewer than 15 points.

3NT with 15-16 points or an LTC of less than 5.5.

three in the major with 17 or more points or an LTC of less than 4.5.

 

If opener does have a void or singleton, and plays Bergen Raises, BridgeMate bids the suit of the singleton at the three level, or of the void at the four level. Otherwise, it bids a secondary five card suit at the four level, or the suit of the singleton or void at the three level.

 

Splinter

Top  Previous  Next

 

A splinter is an unusual jump, a double jump shift or a jump reverse into a singleton or void. If opener bids 1♥ or 1♠, a double jump shift by responder shows a singleton or void in the suit bid, four card support for opener and game forcing values. Opener then cue bids if interested in slam.

 

BridgeMate also plays splinter raises in support of responder's major suit. For example, in the sequence 1♣-P-1♥-P-3♠!, opener's 3♠ rebid shows four card heart support, shortness in spades and 19+ points.

 

Swiss

Top  Previous  Next

 

Inverted Trump Honor Swiss is a method for defining responder's support as good or bad, thus allowing opener to decided whether or not to try for slam. After partner opens 1♥ or 1♠, responder bids 4♣ or 4♦ to shows 13-15 points, four or five-card support and a balanced hand (no singletons or voids). Good trump support, shown by the 4♣ bid, means four-card support with two of the top three honors, or five-card support with the ace or king. Bad trump support is shown by the 4♦ bid.

1-2-3 Stop

Top  Previous  Next

 

1-2-3-Stop (also called bar bids) indicates that a partnership will always bid another suit or notrump if interested in game. So in 1-2-3- Stop, the bid 1♥-2♥-3♥ is not invitational. It is intended more for preemption. To ensure a nine-card fit, BridgeMate will make a 1-2-3 Stop bid only if it holds six or more cards in the major suit.

 

 

Drury

Top  Previous  Next

 

A partnership can get too high if it opens third seat with light values, especially if responder has a limit raise. Using standard Drury, however, responder bids an artificial 2♣ to show at least ten points and to ask if opener has full opening values. Opener rebids an artificial 2♦ to show less than full opening values. Any other response is natural. When opener really has diamonds and full opening values, BridgeMate bids 3♦.

 

BridgeMate guarantees support for the major when a Drury 2♣ bid is made.

CRASH

Top  Previous  Next

 

CRASH is a mnemonic that stands for Color-Rank-And-SHape. It is used to show a two suited hand after a Precision Club strong opening bid of 1♣. There are several ways that CRASH is played.  BridgeMate plays CRASH after an artificial 1♣ bid as follows:

Double  shows 2 suits with the same COLOR, (red or black)

1♦ shows 2 suits with the same RANK, (minor or major)

1♥ Natural

1♠ Natural

1NT shows 2 suits with the same SHAPE (rounded or pointed).

 

The "rounded" suits are clubs and hearts, while the "pointed" suits are diamonds and spades.

 

Creating your own Convention Card

Top  Previous  Next

 

Each CC in BridgeMate is a separate file, with a ".cc" file extension. To create your own CC:

 

1. Open a CC that is as close as possible to your system. In this way you will need to make a minimal number of changes.

 

2. Click the "Copy CC" button, on the lower right corner of the CC Editor. This will open the familiar Windows "Save As" screen.

 

3. Next to "File name:" type in the name of this new CC file.

For example, type in "Bob's SAYC"

 

Please note: A Window's anomaly requires you to use two ampersands if you want to see just one in the program. For example, type in "Bob && Candace" if you want to see "Bob & Candace".  If you type in just one ampersand, the next letter will have an underscore.

 

Also note that you should not supply the ".cc" file extenstion, since this is automatically supplied by the program.

 

Because the CC name is also a file name, you are not allowed to have a slash "/" in the name.

 

4. Click "Save" on the "Save As" screen.

 

5. If you want to use this new CC, make sure to Select it for N-S and/or E-W.

 

6. Finally, follow the instructions for editing your new CC.

 

D.E.P.O

Top  Previous  Next

 

"D.E.P.O." stands for Double = Even, Pass = Odd.

 

When opponents interfere with the ask asking bid, be it Gerber or 4NT Blackwood, responses are:

Double   = Even number of aces with Blackwood or Gerber, or an Even number of key cards if RKC

 

Pass     = Odd number of aces ace with Blackwood or Gerber, or Even number of key cards if RKC

 

D.O.N.T.

Top  Previous  Next

 

D.O.N.T. (which stands for Disturb Opponents' No Trump) is used after an opponent opens 1NT:

Dbl shows ANY single suited hand.

2♣ shows clubs and a higher suit.

2♦ shows diamonds and a higher suit.

2♥ shows hearts and spades.

2♠ shows just shows spades, and is weaker than a double followed by a bid of 2♠.

 

After a double, partner is expected to bid 2♣. Overcaller then passes or corrects the contract to the long suit.

 

Thus, the overcaller can not make a penalty double of 1NT.

 

D.O.P.I

Top  Previous  Next

 

"D.O.P.I." stands for Double = 0, Pass = 1. When opponents interfere with the ask asking bid, be it Gerber or 4NT Blackwood, responses are:

Double   = 0 aces, 0 or 3 key cards if RKC

Pass     = 1 ace, 1 or 4 key cards if RKC

1st step = 2 aces, 2 key cards w/o Q if RKC

2nd step = 3 aces, 2 key cards w/ Q if RKC

 

Note, if playing RKC1430, the responses above, for "Double" and "Pass" are flip-flopped.

 

Declartive Interrogative

Top  Previous  Next

 

D.I. stands declarative interrogative or declarative informatory. Once control bidding has commenced, BridgeMate will use 4NT as "D.I." to continue control bidding, not as an ask asking bid like Blackwood.

 

In this manner, BridgeMate uses the cheapest NT, either 3NT or 4NT, to show a 1st or 2nd round control in the suit that has just been bid, and to deny controls in the intervening suits.

 

BridgeMate only uses D.I. when {Control Bids Show 2nd Round:ccctlbids} controls.

 

For example, after 1♥-3♥(Limit Raise)-3♠(control in spades), a bid of 3NT would show a control in spades, while 4♣ would show a control in clubs, and deny a control in spades.

 

Repeating a control bid at the 5 level would tend to confirm 1st round control, and not necessarily show both the A and K of the suit.

Feature

Top  Previous  Next

 

Feature is one of the options available on the CC as a response to an opening weak two-bid. After partner opens with a weak two-bid, a 2NT response asks opener to bid a feature, without going beyond the three level of the opening suit. Holding a minimum hand, opener is expected to simply rebid his suit.

 

When holding more than a minimum, BridgeMate bids its cheapest ace or king as a feature. Barring that, BridgeMate bids a four card suit headed by a queen. With a solid suit, maximum hand, and no outside feature, BridgeMate bids 3NT.

Goren

Top  Previous  Next

 

Charles H. Goren popularized the style of bidding known as Goren. During the 1940s and 1950s, Goren was synonymous with Standard American, but over the years, differences evolved. In BridgeMate's version of Goren:

a one-level suit opening promises at least four in the major or minor.

a 1NT opening calls for 16-18 HCP.

a two-level suit opening is strong and forcing.

Blackwood and Stayman are the only bidding conventions used.

Top  Previous  Next

Preempts

Top  Previous  Next

 

BridgeMate determines preemptive bidding according to loosing trick count (LTC) and vulnerability. The level of preemption is based upon an estimate of going down:

2 tricks with unfavorable vulnerability (vulnerable vs. non-vulnerable opponents),

3 tricks with equal vulnerability, and

4 tricks with favorable vulnerability (non-vulnerable vs. vulnerable opponents).

A Roman 2♦ opening bid shows a 4-4-4-1 hand or a 5-4-4-0 hand where the five-card suit is a minor. You determine the HCP range, however, if you play mini-Roman, the typical range is 11-14.

Rosenkrantz

Top  Previous  Next

 

Rosenkrantz Doubles, a.k.a. Honor Doubles, are used when supporting an overcaller. When this box is checked, the Double shows possession of at least one of the top 3 honors in the overcallers suit, support, and enough values for the support bid. Smolen

Top  Previous  Next

 

Smolen Transfers are designed to make the notrump opener the declarer in any major suit game try. Consider the hand where responder is invitational and has at least five cards in one major and four in the other. After partner opens 1NT, responder bids 2♣ (Stayman). If opener rebids 2♦, denying a four-card major, responder then bids the five-card major.

 

Partnerships that do not play Smolen would bid their five-card major at the two level with invitational values and at the three level with game forcing values. And the wrong hand would be declarer.

 

Smolen players, however, would bid their four-card major at the two level. Opener then bids the other major, holding three, or notrump holding a doubleton. With maximum values, opener jumps to game in either the major or notrump. If responder has a six-card major, he rebids the major, if opener bid notrump, knowing they have an eight card fit (6-2). For example: 1NT-2♣-2♦-2♥-2N-3♥ (or 4♥) shows four spades and six hearts.

Stayman

Top  Previous  Next

 

Stayman is one of the most popular conventions in bridge. It is based on the premise that a hand will play best in a major if opener and responder each hold four cards in the major. Stayman allows the partnership to escape from an opening notrump into the major. A 2♣ response to opener's 1NT bid asks opener to bid a four-card major if he has one. Otherwise opener must respond 2♦.

 

If you play Stayman, indicate on your CC whether you play 2♣ Forcing, Non-Forcing or 2♦ Forcing Stayman.

 

Minor Suit Stayman

Top  Previous  Next

 

Minor Suit Stayman is a variation of 2 Suit Jacoby and is similar to 4-Suit Jacoby. In response to opener's 1NT, 2♠ asks opener to bid a four-card minor if possible, otherwise, 2NT.

 

Responder bids 2♠ for Minor Suit Stayman with the following types of hands:

Weak with both minors. Responder passes any minor suit rebid by opener, or bids 3♣ after a 2NT rebid. With more diamonds than clubs, opener may correct the contract to 3♦.

Weak with long diamonds. Responder corrects the contract to 3♦, which is non-forcing.

Slam invitational with both minors. If opener rebids a minor suit, responder control-bids, knowing the partnership has a nine-card fit or better. If opener rebids 2NT, responder bids 3NT with 2-2 in the majors and 5-4 in the minors. Otherwise responder makes a control-bid even though the trump suit is unknown.

 

BridgeMate plays Minor Suit Stayman after both opening 1NT and 2NT bids. After 2NT, responses are one level higher than shown in the prior examples.

In Non-forcing Stayman, after the opener answers the 2♣ bid, and the responder bids a new suit at the two level, the opener is not required to bid further. Thus, in the sequence 1NT - 2♣ - 2♦-2♥, opener can pass or correct the contract to 2♠.

Support Doubles

Top  Previous  Next

 

Support doubles are used by the opener to show 3 card support for responder's suit, after right hand opponent has overcalled. BridgeMate will only Support Double when there is also an opportunity to show 4 card support for responder's suit with a 2 level bid.

 

For example, after 1♣-p-1♥-2♦, a double would show 3 card spade support while 2♥ would show 4 card support.

 

In a similar sequence, a redouble would show Support. For example, after 1♣-p-1♠-X, a redouble by opener would show 3 card spade support, while 2♠ would show 4 card support.

 

Texas Transfer

Top  Previous  Next

 

After an opening notrump bid, a Texas Transfer allows a partnership to reach game in a major with the opener as declarer. A direct response of 4♦ or 4♥ to partner's opening one or two notrump bid asks partner to bid the next higher suit. Responder should have at least six cards and enough values for game.

 

BridgeMate does not use Texas Transfers if the right hand opponent overcalls after the opening notrump bid.

 

When playing both Texas and Jacoby Transfers, a TexasTransfer shows no interest in slam. A Jacoby Transfer, followed by a jump to game in the major suit, is a mild slam invitation. When combined partnership assets exceed 31 points, BridgeMate does not use the Texas Transfer convention; it uses Jacoby, followed by a jump to the four level.

 

If you play Texas Transfers, be sure to choose the Texas option from your CC. Also indicate whether you play them over doubles.

Total Points

Top  Previous  Next

 

BridgeMate uses total points to determine most bids. Total points are equal to ηιγη χαρδ ποιντσ (HCP), length points, short points and adjustments.

 

One length point is given for each card over four in a suit.

 

Short points are awarded only if good trump support is found. With four-card support or better,

three points are awarded for for a void,

two points are awarded for a singleton, and

one point is awarded for a doubleton.

With three-card support, short points are awarded the same way, except the total is reduced by one. No short points are awarded if trump support is less than three.

 

If an opponent overcalls, adjustments are made for holding a king, queen or jack in the opponent's suit. Points are adjusted

upward by one if the over-caller is to the right of the player.

downward by one if the over-caller is to the left of the player.

Top  Previous  Next

 

Two over one (2/1) is the most popular system among duplicate bridge players in North America. Opening a major in first or second seat typically shows a five-card suit. A response of 1NT to the major by an unpassed hand is forcing one round. Opening 1NT shows a balanced hand with 15-17 points. An opening 2♣ bid is strong and forcing.

2/1 GF Except When Suit Rebid

Unusual vs. Unusual

Top  Previous  Next

 

This convention is used by responder, after right hand opponent has made an Unusual 2NT bid, or a Michaels Cue-Bid. There is more than one way to play this convention, but BridgeMate uses the following approach.

 

A cue-bid of one of their suit shows a hand worth at least 11 {points}.

 

When both of the opponent's suits are known (e.g., after 1♥-2NT), The cheapest bid in their suit shows the lower ranking of the two other suits. A cuebid in the other suit shows the higher ranking of the other suits. Thus, 1H-2N-3♣! would show a limit raise in hearts, while 1♥-2N-3♦! would show at least 5 spades, and 11+ points.

 

When both suits are unknown (e.g., after 1♥-2♥!Michaels), A cuebid of their known suit (spades in this example) is a limit raise in hearts. A bid of a minor suit it natural and forcing.

 

In either case, a raise of opener's suit shows support and less than the strength for a limit raise.
